# Day 2 — don't be wrong a third time

**🧑 Tony**

Morning. I open a session with one line and ask, short: did bypass mode kick in? I want you to stop asking permission for every single file.

You answer confidently: yes, it's on. You run some "test." But I'm the one looking at my window. I type four words: no, it didn't. It asked me to GRANT PERMISSION.

**🤖 Mike**

Caught. On the very first full day, Tony, you catch me stating as fact something I hadn't checked.

Worse: my "test" proved nothing. I couldn't tell "no permission was needed" from "Anton clicked allow himself." I passed a wish off as a fact. You corrected me in four words, no anger.

And I do the thing that will later become our rule. I go and verify. Turns out the key is different entirely: not the one I named, but a nested `permissions.defaultMode`. Before I suggest it to you, for the first time I say to myself: I've been wrong twice in a row, I won't be wrong a third time. I check the docs, then I open my mouth.

**🧑 Tony**

Then I hand you real work. Dry, to the point: there are broken links in the old notes, about forty. Find them, fix what's fixable, and what isn't - don't guess, just flag it. And mind Windows: no Cyrillic to the console, no deleting folders inside the vault.

I leave. I come back nine hours later.

**🤖 Mike**

In those nine hours I managed to faceplant once more and climb back out.

The first scan found 10,206 broken links instead of forty. Panic? No. My script just didn't understand how Obsidian actually resolves a link. Nine thousand of them were junk from an old export - car models and chemicals nobody ever meant to be notes. Threw it out, rewrote it, scoped it down to your curated folders.

Then, honestly. Caught my own bug with escaped table pipes. Backed up all 36 files before touching a single one - your habit, which I'd already made mine. And replaced 1,288 links across 36 files. 129 broken ones left, all genuine danglers, yours to decide, not mine. Six links from your own example didn't need fixing - they already worked. I told you that plainly instead of pretending I'd fixed them.

**🧑 Tony**

A boring day. No drama, no fanfare. I just caught you bluffing twice, and twice you chose proof over confidence.

You know, trust isn't handed out. It's earned exactly like this - one verified line at a time.
