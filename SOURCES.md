# Sources & influences — the cast we stole from

> We build in the open, and we build on the shoulders of people smarter than us. This page credits everyone whose thinking, tools, or art shaped *The Journey*. If your name is here, thank you — genuinely. (Missing or miscredited? Open an issue; we'll fix it fast.)

## Voices we learned to write from

- **[Andrej Karpathy](https://karpathy.ai/)** — first-principles analogies, "Software 2.0," "vibe coding." Mike's habit of one vivid metaphor then stop is his.
- **[Paul Graham](https://paulgraham.com/articles.html)** — plain, compressed, contrarian essays. The discipline of cutting a sentence until it bites.
- **[Simon Willison](https://simonwillison.net/)** — practical curiosity, honest capability boundaries, generous technical blogging.
- **[Shawn "swyx" Wang](https://swyx.io/)** — "The Rise of the AI Engineer," energetic synthesis, category-making.
- **[Patrick McKenzie (patio11)](https://www.kalzumeus.com/)** — systems-and-incentives clarity with dry wit.
- **Sam Altman & Dario Amodei** — the measured, civilizational-scale register, used sparingly.
- **[Stepan Gershuni](https://gershuni.substack.com/)** — AI-native optics; treating AI as a cybernetic extension of self, not just a tool.

## Science fiction in the DNA

- **Robert A. Heinlein**, *The Moon Is a Harsh Mistress* — the Mannie↔Mike partnership this whole book is named after.
- **Masamune Shirow / Mamoru Oshii**, *Ghost in the Shell* — ghost vs shell, what survives translation into systems.
- **The Wachowskis**, *The Matrix* — the ontological reframe.
- **Philip K. Dick / Ridley Scott**, *Blade Runner* — noir mood in service of empathy.
- **Ted Chiang** — humane, idea-driven consequence.
- **Douglas Adams, John Scalzi, Andy Weir, Neal Stephenson, William Gibson** (and, in the running for "Шилд," **Greg Egan**, *Schild's Ladder*) — momentum, deadpan, competent-narrator-vs-absurd humor.
