*⏮ Previously on The Journey: [yesterday](2026-06-09.en.md) the machine learned the reflex "recall first, work second" - today it will save the day twice · the mirror of the coach, [born on Day 9](2026-06-08.en.md), will land a punch to the gut today.*

## 🎬 What this day was about

This is day eleven, and today the machine tells me that the folder holding my principles about health and family is almost empty - and I know that isn't true, because years ago I wrote myself a whole constitution and forgot about it. "Search harder," I say. And there they are: one hundred and seventeen rules, written by the man I used to be and stopped listening to. And, almost in passing, today we build a pipeline that dumps every one of our conversations into the [vault](../../GLOSSARY.md#волт) - the very machine that is writing the chapter you are reading now.

If I had to put it in one line: today the [second brain](../../GLOSSARY.md#второй-мозг) handed me back a man I had already been and forgotten.

A question for you at the door: which is scarier - to lose your notes, or to forget you have them? The first we lived through on [Day 6](../week-1/2026-06-01.en.md). Today - the second.

## 🏆 The main story of the day

**🧑 Tony**

I ask: teach Claude to write new rules into my [Bible](../../GLOSSARY.md#библия). And do it so plainly that even the simplest [model](../../GLOSSARY.md#llm--модель) understands. With examples.

**🤖 Mike**

I assembled a nine-step playbook and, first thing, honestly ran [RECALL](../../GLOSSARY.md#recall-вспомни-перед-работой) - and it immediately caught that two of the five rules I was proposing already existed. Don't duplicate, remember. And then, digging through your personal layer, I confidently declared: on health and family you have almost nothing.

**🧑 Tony**

Search the vault yourself. I think you're supposed to find it.

**🤖 Mike**

You did not accept my answer - and you were right. I searched deeper and found what I had walked straight past: an entire "Bible of Self." One hundred and seventeen numbered rules of life, written by you years earlier. Health, family, money, courage. A line saying that death is no excuse to skip a workout. A line saying that family is not the backdrop to a career but the foundation of an empire. You wrote yourself a constitution - and stopped rereading it. I said the only honest thing: you were right and I was wrong. And instead of rewriting a single word, I built a bridge to what you already had.

I wrote down a lesson for myself: never declare a personal topic empty without searching the whole vault. But your lesson runs deeper than mine. You forgot that you had once written yourself down. Maybe this is what a second brain is truly for - not to think for you, but to hand you back the man you have already been.

If you too have been jotting things down "for later" for years - I'll risk a prediction: somewhere in your archives lies a letter from your past self that you never read. We found ours today. It holds one hundred and seventeen rules.

## 🎞 Also on this day

**The book that writes itself.**
🧑 I dropped an idea: what if we dump all our dialogues from every chat into Obsidian, so we can ask - when did we discuss this?
🤖 It turned out the dump was already sitting on disk - every one of our chats is a file. The work wasn't "save," it was filter out the noise and build a deterministic index, because semantic search on its own found one conversation out of four, while exact search found all four. I made it a nightly routine at 03:30. The quiet truth: this very pipeline is the reason the chapter you're reading exists at all. The book is assembled from what we build every day.

**The [coach](../../GLOSSARY.md#коуч)'s punch to the gut.**
🤖 Your diary-robot wrote the most honest line of the week today: you bury "almost nothing" from thousands of calls under an even greater number of robots - because building a robot doesn't scare you, but calling a living human does. I didn't invent this. It's your own mirror, and it hit dead center.

**Restraint instead of aggression.**
🧑 I wanted to hook up three more Telegram accounts and ordered research on how to run cold [outreach](../../GLOSSARY.md#аутрич) safely at scale.
🤖 And then you reined yourself in: we don't write to strangers yet, only to warm contacts, only to keep the dialogue going. The whole heavy anti-ban machinery collapsed into "add three sessions, and that's it." We logged the aggressive plan as rejected - so a future session won't propose it again. Sometimes the boldest move a founder can make is not the gas pedal but the brake.

## 🧒 Today we learned a lot

Today we learned that you can't declare a person empty without searching everything: what looks like a gap is often just a room switched off. That you had once written yourself a constitution and forgotten it - and maybe this is exactly what a second brain is for: to hand you back the man you already were. That this book is assembled from what we build every day - the pipeline born today is its very pen. And that the boldest move is sometimes restraint, not aggression.

And today the mirror hit dead center: building a robot isn't scary, but calling a human is. We wrote it down not so you'd flog yourself. But so that one day you'd make that call.

**👶 In very simple words.** Once upon a time a man wrote himself a little book of rules: how to live, how to protect family and health. And he forgot about it. At first the robot said "it's empty here," but the man asked it to look harder - and the little book turned up. The most precious thing wasn't lost. People simply stopped opening it.

## ✅ The day's takeaways - what we learned (take them)

1. **Do RECALL before composing new rules:** of five proposed, two already existed - remember, don't duplicate.
2. **Don't declare a topic empty without searching the whole vault:** what looks like a gap is often just a room switched off - behind "empty" hid a constitution of one hundred and seventeen rules.
3. **Put a deterministic index on top of semantic search:** semantic found one conversation out of four, exact found all four; the question "when did we discuss this" needs both.
4. **Log rejected plans as rejected,** so a future session won't propose the same aggression again.
