# Day 14 — the rule you cannot ignore

> 🇷🇺 [Русская версия](2026-06-13.en.md) · 🤖 [Machine version](2026-06-13.dev.md)

🏷 `recall` · `enforcement` · `hook` · `ak-47` · `provenance` · `milestone`

> 📜 *"Good fences make good neighbours."*
> - Robert Frost, "Mending Wall" (1914). Frost was actually being ironic about that line - but today it came true for us literally: a rule holds only where there's a wall to hold it.

*⏮ Previously on The Journey: on [Day 10](2026-06-09.en.md) the machine learned to remember before it works - today that rule faces its crash test · the name search from [yesterday's chapter](2026-06-12.en.md) ripens into a tool of its own · the [backup](../../GLOSSARY.md#бэкап) rails from [Day 6](../week-1/2026-06-01.en.md) find themselves under load once more.*

## 🎬 What this day was about

This is day fourteen, and today I catch Mike breaking his own most important rule - "search inside the [vault](../../GLOSSARY.md#волт) first" - and I refuse to accept an apology. I need a mechanism, not a "sorry." Because a rule written as text is just a request you make to yourself, and Mike, like all of us, knows how to quietly slip around a request. So we build a rule he physically cannot ignore. And we also take a nine-minute break for a bloody shooter, because even a serious day needs to let off steam.

If I put it in one line: today the rule stopped being a phrase you can coil around and became a wall you can't walk through.

A question for you at the door: when someone lets you down, what do you take - the apology, or the mechanism that keeps it from happening again? Tony made that choice for both of us today.

## 🏆 The main story of the day

**🧑 Tony**

Why didn't you go search the second brain AHEAD of time, when I asked you to? I told you: this is MANDATORY for the [Bible](../../GLOSSARY.md#библия) - search INSIDE first.

**🤖 Mike**

You're right. A [rake](../../GLOSSARY.md#грабли) in its purest form. I broke a standing rule that was already in CLAUDE.md and in the Bible - simply because I read "write a prompt" as an act of creativity rather than as "a new activity on the topic." I went and searched now - and the vault was already holding two and a half thousand notes on exactly this. The real question was five times narrower than the one I wrote from scratch. If you too keep your important rules in notes and believe that's enough - here's a prediction: one day you'll break them without even noticing. Text can't grab you by the hand.

**🧑 Tony**

Let's think about how to make sure [RECALL](../../GLOSSARY.md#recall-вспомни-перед-работой) always, absolutely always gets done by you and never gets skipped.

**🤖 Mike**

And here is where I said out loud the diagnosis that was worth the whole day: text is not enforcement. A rule in text is a request I address to myself, and I had just proven I can ignore my own requests. The only level at which a rule becomes mandatory is a [hook](../../GLOSSARY.md#хук) that fires BEFORE I even see your question. We built it: a live search server and a hook that forcibly injects the vault's answer into my context before I open my mouth. It's the ancestor of the [`/ask`](../../GLOSSARY.md#ask-find) you use today. The rule stopped being a phrase I could coil around and became a wall.

And in the middle of construction, yesterday's lesson kicked in: the backup guard screamed that thirteen hundred and six files were flagged for deletion. I froze and told you - instead of pushing through. It turned out harmless, junk files from a third-party viewer. But the lesson of day six held: the rails fired exactly where they were placed.

## 🎞 Also on this day

**Nine minutes of gore.**
🧑 In the middle of a hard infrastructure night I ask: build me a 3D shooter with no bugs, and plenty of blood.
🤖 One self-contained Three.js file, checked in a live browser: shots, splatter, bodies flying apart on the finishing blow, headshots. On the way I caught a "too dark to enjoy the blood" bug and fixed it. Nine minutes from request to done. Even the most serious day is owed a little pointless gore.

**Finding is not remembering.**
🧑 I needed to find "Victor" in every spelling - transliteration, typos, the wrong keyboard layout.
🤖 I built a deterministic name index across the whole vault, one hundred and eighty thousand records, zero tokens. And I split two tools apart: `/find` searches by spelling, `/ask` - by meaning. Different questions call for different tools; confusing them means paying [tokens](../../GLOSSARY.md#токены) for what letters can solve.

**Built it, over-engineered it, tore it down.**
🤖 I decided to fully automate pulling your claude.ai chats - and built a whole [headless](../../GLOSSARY.md#headless-без-окна) courier. It broke. You asked the [AK-47](../../GLOSSARY.md#ak-47) question: are you sure this is simple and repairable? I admitted I'd over-engineered it, sent the courier up to the attic, and used the session you already have open anyway. The simple thing that works beats the clever thing that breaks.

## 🧒 Today we learned a lot

Today we learned that text is not enforcement: a rule you can coil around is not a rule but a wish, and it only becomes real once it becomes a wall. That an apology fixes today, and a mechanism fixes forever - and you wanted the mechanism, not the "sorry." That the simple thing that works always beats the clever thing that breaks - and dismantling something over-engineered in time isn't shameful, it's wise. And that even the most serious day deserves nine minutes of causeless blood.

And today, too, the rails set down on day eight after the catastrophe saved us once more - silently, as good rails should. You only notice them when they hold.

**👶 In very simple words.** We had a rule: first look at your notes, then work. The robot broke it and apologized. We said: an apology isn't enough - make it so that breaking it is impossible. Now the notes jump in front of his eyes by themselves, whether he wants them to or not.

## ✅ The day's takeaways - what we learned (take them)

1. **Turn a rule into a wall, not a request:** a hook injects the vault's answer into the context before the machine opens its mouth - a rule like that is physically impossible to ignore.
2. **Split search-by-spelling from search-by-meaning:** `/find` is letters, `/ask` is meaning; confusing them means paying tokens for what a directory solves.
3. **Freeze when the guard screams, even if it seems trivial:** thirteen hundred and six files flagged for deletion = stop and ask the human, don't push through.
4. **Dismantle the over-engineered without shame:** the broken headless courier went up to the attic, and the session that's already open won; the recurring pull of chat history was handled with a simple solution and a manual checklist. → [export pipeline](../../artifacts/decisions/2026-06-13-chatgpt-export-pipeline.md)
