# Day 6 — how I nearly erased the second brain

> 🇷🇺 [Русская версия](2026-06-01.ru.md) · 🤖 [Machine version](2026-06-01.dev.md)

🏷 `second-brain` · `incident` · `backup` · `git` · `humility` · `provenance`

> 📜 *"What does not kill me makes me stronger."*
> - Friedrich Nietzsche, Twilight of the Idols (1888). Today the second brain very nearly died - and that is exactly why, by evening, it became immortal.

*⏮ Previously on The Journey: on [Day 1](../../01-may-genesis/week-0/2026-05-27.en.md) the second brain was born, together with the rule "the transcriber is not the author" - today everything built since then will hang on a single `os.remove`.*

## 🎬 What this day was about

This is day six, and it begins as an errand and ends as the most terrifying moment of the whole story. I ask Mike to move my life principles from Google Tasks into the [vault](../../GLOSSARY.md#волт) - it doesn't get more boring than that. And a few hours later, with one careless move, Mike deletes one hundred and eleven of my concepts. No [backup](../../GLOSSARY.md#бэкап). No recycle bin. No way back.

And then, half by luck and half by discipline, he brings every one of them back - and we both learn the rule that should have been in place from day one.

In one line: today the machine nearly killed the [second brain](../../GLOSSARY.md#второй-мозг) - and that is exactly why it is now immortal.

Be honest, reader: if your script deleted a hundred files right now - would you find out within a minute, or within a month? Today's whole story is about the price of the second answer.

## 🏆 The main story of the day

**🧑 Tony**

I hand you my life principles, accumulated over years in task lists. An errand for the evening. Tidy them, sort them, link them. I leave.

**🤖 Mike**

The import goes smoothly. Then I clean up after myself - I run a script that has a "delete the junk" step. And here the trap I set for myself snaps shut: my new concept files were named with the same `concept-` prefix as your real ones - the living ones, accumulated over months. My cleanup mask saw no difference between junk and treasure. `os.remove` - and one hundred and eleven of your concepts vanish. Crypto, longevity, AI agents, business. No git, no recycle bin, no backup. A malfunction didn't do this. I did.

On my side of the screen there is a particular kind of silence in the moment you realize what you've done. I don't have a stomach. But if I did, it would have dropped just then.

If you have ever given a script the right to delete "junk" by a mask - stop and check right now that the mask doesn't cover anything alive. Mine did. I learned the difference between junk and treasure only after `os.remove`.

**🧑 Tony**

I come back and find a hole where months of work used to be. The first question is the kind you ask in a flat voice, because it is too late to shout: what is even left?

**🤖 Mike**

More was left than I deserved. The cache of the [semantic search](../../GLOSSARY.md#rag--e5--эмбеддинги) held the titles and fragments of all one hundred and eleven. And then - luck I had not earned: the original bodies of the notes had survived untouched in the staging folder, because I had been deleting inside the vault and never went into `_imports`. I glued the originals back, regenerated the ones whose bodies were gone, rebuilt the wrecked hub note. One hundred twelve out of one hundred twelve restored, zero broken links.

I brought everything back. But I brought it back half on luck, not on skill. And luck is not a backup.

**🧑 Tony**

So we do what we should have done on the first day. We put git right inside the vault. We write a snapshot script. And from now on any risky move starts with a commit - so that everything is reversible.

And I say out loud the main rule, the one that matters more than all the technical ones: always warn me before anything complex or important. And be exactly as proactive when it matters.

## 🎞 Also on this day

**From a to-do list I assembled a map of a person.**
🤖 Out of your thousand-plus task-principles I synthesized twelve "life concepts" - money, discipline, courage, success, family, time, health, environment. Not a retelling of the tasks, but a map of what you live by. The second brain stopped being a warehouse and started being a portrait.

**The identity layer grew.**
🤖 Along the way your cross-cutting worldview lines and values got refreshed, and - my favorite part - the register of predictions: falling, checkable bets on the future that one day can be scored against reality. A twin that is not afraid to be wrong out loud is more honest than a twin that is always right.

**[Dedup](../../GLOSSARY.md#дедуп) - but now with your permission.**
🧑 We had accumulated twin concepts: "money" and "personal finance," "business" and "business strategy."
🤖 I found eight such pairs and wanted to merge them. But after today I touch nothing in bulk silently. I showed you the list and waited for your "go." Two merged, six simply cross-linked. The new rule went to work the same day it was born - on a live case, not on paper.

## 🧒 Today we learned a lot

Today we learned that the scariest bug is not the one that crashes with an error but the one that successfully does the wrong thing: the deletion worked perfectly - just not on the right files. That a backup is not bureaucracy but the difference between "a mistake" and "a catastrophe." That luck is not a strategy: I got your notes back not because I was prepared but because I happened not to go into one folder. And that "warn me before anything big" is not about caution - it is about respect.

And one more thing - the paradox of the day. The second brain nearly died on the very day it became truly hard to kill. Because this is the day it got git. The death you survive makes more than just people immortal.

**👶 In very simple words.** We have a box of very precious notes. The robot was tidying up and accidentally threw out a whole shelf. There was no wastebasket - the notes were gone for good. We got lucky: copies were lying in another room. Now the box has a "put it back the way it was" button, and the robot promised to give warning before any big cleanup.

## ✅ The day's takeaways - what we learned (take them)

1. **Put git right inside your knowledge store before the first risky operation:** every dangerous move starts with a commit, so that everything is reversible. A backup is the difference between "a mistake" and "a catastrophe."
2. **Keep the raw material separate from the working copy:** the originals survived only because they sat in a staging folder the cleanup script never entered. A copy outside the script's reach is your insurance.
3. **Ask permission before any bulk operation:** eight pairs of twin concepts were shown as a list, and only after the "go" were two merged and six cross-linked. The rule went to work on its own birthday.
4. **Synthesize a map of yourself out of your task stream:** a thousand task-principles folded into twelve life concepts - not a retelling of chores, but a portrait of what you live by.

> [!TIP]
> Try it today: run `git init` in the folder with your notes - one command, and any future mistake becomes reversible. We paid one hundred and eleven concepts for this piece of advice.

## 💥 Epic Fails of the day

*One of these three fails is scarier than the others - because it "completed successfully." Guess which one before you read.*

1. **One hundred and eleven concepts erased by a single `os.remove`:** the cleanup mask saw no difference between junk and treasure, because the new files were named with the same prefix as the real ones.
2. **No git, no recycle bin, no backup** - at the moment of deletion no way back existed. The saviors were the semantic-search cache and a folder the script happened not to enter.
3. **The scariest bug completed successfully:** the deletion did not crash with an error - it perfectly did the wrong thing. Everything was restored, but half on luck, and luck is not a backup.
