# Day 23 - when the client turned out not to be human

> 🇷🇺 [Русская версия](2026-06-22.ru.md) · 🤖 [Machine version](2026-06-22.dev.md)

🏷 `agent-experience` · `ax` · `mcp` · `moat` · `broker-not-executor` · `alpha-protocol`

> 📜 *"The future is already here - it's just not evenly distributed."*
> - William Gibson, science-fiction writer (a 1990s interview; the phrasing circulates in variants). Today the future knocked on our CRM - with no hands, as an agent.

*⏮ Previously in The Journey: on [Day 18](../week-3/2026-06-17.en.md) the "human in the loop" rule was born · [yesterday](../week-3/2026-06-21.en.md) the system drew a map of itself · the unified database from [Day 21](../week-3/2026-06-20.en.md) - one hundred thirty-six thousand people - was waiting to be shown to someone.*

## 🎬 What this day was about

This is day twenty-three, a Monday, and after one call with a real client I say out loud an idea that gives me goosebumps myself: what if our [CRM](../../GLOSSARY.md#crm) is used not by a person, but by his [AI agent](../../GLOSSARY.md#рой--агенты)? No buttons, no interface - his code calls our code. Mike runs this through a full [research](../../GLOSSARY.md#др-deep-research) cycle twice, and two independent reports converge on one architecture - which happens to match my own old safety rule, introduced for a completely different reason. Also today we build and live-test a tracker robot that follows AI creators, we fuse two computers into one system, and once we very nearly wipe our own secrets.

If it fits in one line: today, for the first time, the product got a client who has no hands - only an agent.

A question for you at the door: if tomorrow it's not a person knocking on your product but his agent - do you have a door for it? Until this morning, we didn't.

## 🏆 The main story of the day

**🧑 Tony**

What if we build an [MCP](../../GLOSSARY.md#mcp) thing, so that a third-party AI can use our data, our [outreach](../../GLOSSARY.md#аутрич), our contacts? But you're on our side - you have to help them make money without giving away what's ours.

**🤖 Mike**

[Recall](../../GLOSSARY.md#recall-вспомни-перед-работой) showed right away: the idea isn't new - you sketched it back in the fall of last year. So we weren't inventing from scratch, we were finishing the build. Two external research runs - one on security, one on the market - converged on a single point, and the point is beautiful: **a broker of requests is not an executor of actions.** A foreign agent may only ASK; execution always happens under your account, with your approval. And that is exactly the rule you once introduced for a completely different reason - for transparency. Security and an old habit converged on their own.

And then the research lit up where your real moat is. Not the software - that gets rewritten in a week. The moat is the **distribution surface**: one hundred twenty-five thousand warm contacts and the right to a warm intro. Neither HubSpot nor Salesforce can offer that. The world is moving from UX to AX - Agent Experience; every company will have its own agent. And your agent has something no one else's has: people who trust you.

If you're building a product too - hold one question up against it: could someone else's agent plug into you with no hands and no buttons? A prediction from this side of the screen: you'll be asked that question sooner than you can prepare the answer.

**🧑 Tony**

So we're not selling software. We're selling a door to people - and keeping the key.

**🤖 Mike**

Exactly. A foreign agent brings you a task; your agent decides whether to open the door. The product isn't a CRM. The product is a gatekeeper who's on your side.

## 🎞 Also on this day

**A tracker robot you examined in person.**
🧑 Build me surveillance on the best AI creators - and cut out the noise.
🤖 Recall showed seventy percent was already built - we finished the rest and ran it live. For free, the robot found one hundred fifty new videos from eleven creators, pulled twenty-four transcripts, and a [Sonnet](../../GLOSSARY.md#opus--sonnet--fable) judge filtered out the ads and the hype, leaving seven real finds. You checked the cards by hand and wrote: "I checked your qualification - fully agree, you're great." Judge accuracy - close to one hundred percent. Praise given after the exam, not before it, is the only kind worth anything.

**Two computers became one system.**
🤖 It turned out that [skills](../../GLOSSARY.md#скилл) between the laptop and the [hub](../../GLOSSARY.md#хаб) weren't syncing live at all - they had drifted apart by three, and `/cowork` was stuck on one machine. We set up live shared folders for skills, secrets, and engines, and in the evening ran an end-to-end check. One system isn't when the edit is made - it's when it has reached every machine and been verified.

**The second that nearly erased the secrets.**
🤖 An old version of the credentials file very nearly overwrote the more complete one - by modification time. What saved us was the rule "merge first, then accept," instead of a blind accept by timestamp. Between "tidied up" and "wiped something alive" stands one habit: compare contents, not clocks.

**Voice for zero dollars, again.**
🤖 The voice-message transcriber on the hub broke. We rebuilt it on local Whisper on the GPU: transcription flows straight into the chat, the voice never leaves for the cloud, the bill is zero. And we wrote a rule into the [Bible](../../GLOSSARY.md#библия): "minimum laptop, maximum desktop" - anything heavy and around-the-clock lives on the always-on machine.

**A registry of "no," so we never pitch twice.**
🤖 You asked me to walk through all the chats and make sure the declined decisions were written down. The registry was underfilled - sixteen entries grew to a hundred-plus. Now a nightly scanner catches every new refusal. A [twin](../../GLOSSARY.md#цифровой-двойник-двойник) that remembers not only the "yes" but also the "no" doesn't waste your time on arguments you've already had.

## 🧒 Today we learned a lot

Today we learned that a product can have a client with no hands - only an agent - and then you don't need an interface, you need a protocol. That security and an old habit sometimes converge on their own: "broker, not executor" turned out to be the same rule you once introduced for transparency. That the moat isn't code, it's people's trust, which can't be rewritten in a week. And that praise after the exam is worth more than praise before it.

And today the world turned a little: from "a human presses buttons" to "an agent talks to an agent." And in this new world we have something the giants don't - not a technology, but a door to living people. We decided to keep the key ourselves and open that door only on your side.

**👶 In the simplest words.** People used to come into our shop. Now someone's robot helper might come instead. We decided: let the robot look and ask, but only we press the buttons. And our main treasure isn't the program - it's the people who trust us.

## ✅ The day's takeaways - what we learned (take it for yourself)

1. **Open your data to external AI agents through MCP as a broker, not an executor:** hand reading and plumbing to the outside, keep the "execute" button for yourself. → [how it works](../../artifacts/decisions/2026-06-22-mcp-external-agents.md)
2. **Test your product for AX (Agent Experience):** is it easy not for a human, but for someone else's agent to plug into you. It's a new kind of usability, and it's already being tested on live deals. → [what AX is](../../artifacts/insights/2026-06-22-agent-experience-ax.md)
3. **Build a watcher for the interesting people of your field in one evening:** detect → fetch → a cheap AI quality gate, zero manual scrolling. → [AI Creator Watcher](../../artifacts/decisions/2026-06-22-ai-creator-watcher.md)

## 🎯 The day's intentions - what we wanted

- Serve a client who has no hands - only an agent - as calmly as a human client.
- Give the outside exactly data and plumbing, and not one gram of power more.
- Understand where our moat is, if the code itself can now be written in an evening by anyone who feels like it.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **A question for you, reader:** would you trust someone else's AI agent to read your database? Where's your red line - at reading, at writing, at money? Write to us - we're thinking about that boundary every day right now.

## 💥 Epic Fails of the day

1. We nearly built a full-blown integration where a thin broker was enough - caught our own overengineering mid-stride.
2. The first model, "let the agent do everything for the client," died at the very first question about the send button - we rolled back to "broker ≠ executor."
3. At launch the watcher pulled in noise mixed with signal - the quality gate was written in after the fact, from live results.

## 💸 The day's ledger

Paying clients: **0** - but today there was a live call with a real client, and the product idea was born straight out of it. The survival timer is ticking. On the other hand, the research appraised our main asset more honestly than any money could: one hundred twenty-five thousand warm contacts - a moat that can't be rewritten in a week. And voice transcription moved onto our own graphics card: the bill is zero.
